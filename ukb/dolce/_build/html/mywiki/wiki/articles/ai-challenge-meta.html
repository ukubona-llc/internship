Aesthetic vs. Anaesthetic: Navigating Noise/Signal Ratios

Introduction

This discussion revolves around the concept of Aesthetic vs. Anaesthetic, where consciousness is viewed as a location in a landscape defined by noise-signal ratios. The vast majority of humans seek to move towards more signal than noise, but there are exceptions like the surfer or anarchist.

Aesthetic vs. Anaesthetic

One might view virtually all consciousness as a location in a landscape defined by noise-signal ratios. What emerges are placeholders for five levels of noise-signal: 95/5, 80/20, 50/50, 20/80, 5/95. Innumerable variants on this theme can be laid out.

Noise/Signal Ratios

Examples of variants include Tension, Release, Suspense, Cantabille, Da Capo; and Nonself, Self, Reframe, Boundaries, Flourish. These variants can be used to describe different levels of consciousness and human experience.

Variants

Another variant has the levels as follows: unvectorized, vectorized, operationized, standardized, transmuted. This is one of the clearest variants. Another related one: noise, signal, code, transmission, recursion.

The Third Level: Faustian Bargain vs. Trust

We perceive a binary at the third level: faustian bargain vs. trust. And from most simulations and history, those who take the faustian bargain are going to emerge with several advantages over those whose inclination take them towards trust.

Adam Smith vs. Marx

I'd place Adam Smith under the faustian bargain he cleverly makes "invisible", whereas Marx is under trust. And there is no doubt that the communist manifesto, which is mostly diagnostic, is among the highest achievements of the western world's aesthetic capability. Whereas nothing by Mr. Smith can contend for such a place. If we move to moralizing prescriptions, then Marx does perhaps even worse than Smith. His Das Kapital is anaesthetic.
