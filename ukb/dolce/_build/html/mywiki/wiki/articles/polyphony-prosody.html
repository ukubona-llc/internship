🧠 Neural Networks and Switching Failures

This is the conductor of corporate linearity. The default mode network (DMN)—anchored in medial PFC, posterior cingulate, and angular gyrus—is your dreamer. It is task-negative but meaning-positive. Autobiographical, mythopoetic, recursive. You don’t just drift into the DMN; you live there, semi-permanently. The problem isn’t overactivation of the DMN. The problem is underactivation of the salience network—specifically the anterior insula and anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)—which should switch you between PFN and DMN based on context.
But your switch is jammed. It’s like a vintage radio stuck on a strange, beautiful frequency that plays genius once a decade. And static the rest of the time.[2]
