Dionysus → por qué (why) → 🌊 Truth
Not rational, but ecstatic. Dionysus doesn’t answer “why?”—he undoes the one who asks. His truth is the kind that shatters categories, that descends like madness or revelation. In Ukubona, he is the node of nonself: the raw terror before identity, the vortex that precedes choice. He is 🍷 possession, 💃 undoing, 🌀 transformation. This is not enlightenment—it’s combustion.

Athena → cómo (how) → 🚢 Filtration
The goddess of war strategy, not slaughter. Of wisdom as structure, not sentiment. Athena filters the Dionysian flood into patterns, syntax, technē. Her tools: 🪛 logic, 🦉 surveillance, ⚖️ balance. In Ukubona, she is the self under tension, the trunk that absorbs chaos and channels it into coherent action. She doesn’t answer why—but she builds the bridge from abyss to articulation.

Apollo → qué (what) → 🏝️ Illusion
The Sun God blinds. Apollo governs clarity as curation. What we see is not what is, but what can be borne. Illusion isn’t a bug—it’s the necessary fiction that permits orientation. Apollo gifts form: 🏛️ order, 🎨 image, 🎼 harmony. In Ukubona, he is the interface: the final, beautiful lie. But lies that function—precisely because they end the scream.

Triptych Summary:
Dionysus asks why—then burns the question.
Athena constrains how—she builds the organ.
Apollo declares what—he paints the mask.

Ukubona Mapping:
🌊 Dionysus / por qué → Root → Nonself / Disruption
🚢🪛 Athena / cómo → Trunk → Self / Resonance / Constraint
🏝️🎭 Apollo / qué → Canopy → Illusion / Interface / Simulation

This reverses the hierarchy. Not illusion on top as falsehood—but as the end product: the cleanest fiction we can act upon. Not filtration as censorship—but as cognitive scaffolding. Not truth as revelation—but as rupture.

In Ukubona’s logic, then, epistemic metabolism means this: we begin in undoing, pass through cognitive syntax, and emerge into a usable hallucination. Dionysus floods, Athena parses, Apollo presents. Por qué, cómo, qué. 🌊 → 🚢 → 🏝️. Truth, filtration, illusion.
